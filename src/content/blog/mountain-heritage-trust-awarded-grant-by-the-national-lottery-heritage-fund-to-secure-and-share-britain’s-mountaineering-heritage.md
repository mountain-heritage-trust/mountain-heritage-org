For 25 years the Mountain Heritage Trust has been dedicated to preserving and curating one of the UK's most important collections relating to climbing, mountaineering and exploration. The collection contains photographs, archives, artefacts and personal records that document more than a century of British and international mountaineering history. Without this funding from the Heritage Fund the stories and treasures within this collection might never reach the audiences they deserve.

The project will improve access to these collections through a programme of digitisation, interpretation and public engagement. New exhibitions and displays will be developed with partner organisations, alongside learning resources. These will include oral history work, community engagement activity and environmental heritage content that explores changing mountain landscapes.

A major focus of the project will be widening participation and engaging new audiences, including young people, schools, community groups and those who may not previously have connected with mountaineering heritage.

The project will also strengthen the long-term resilience of the Trust through volunteer development, partnership working, audience growth and new approaches to fundraising and income generation. The project will protect and share this important collection for future generations while preserving the legacy of mountaineering’s greatest adventurers and pioneers.

[Julian Duxfield](https://www.mountain-heritage.org/team/julian-duxfield/), Chair of the Mountain Heritage Trust, said:

"We are delighted to have received this support from The National Lottery Heritage Fund. Thanks to National Lottery players, we will be able to protect, share and celebrate Britain’s rich mountaineering heritage with many more people across the UK. This funding will help us improve access to our collections, develop new partnerships, support volunteers and ensure these remarkable stories continue to inspire future generations."
