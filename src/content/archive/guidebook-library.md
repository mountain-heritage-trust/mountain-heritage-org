Our guidebook library covers almost all of the UK, alongside many rare and out-of-print guidebooks documenting how routes and crags have been recorded over time.

From early club guides to modern definitive volumes, the collection traces the development of climbing across the country — the discovery of new crags, the evolution of grading, and the changing conventions of how routes are described. Many of the earlier and rarer editions are difficult to find elsewhere.

The library is available to researchers by appointment. To arrange a visit, email [enquiries@mountain-heritage.org](mailto:enquiries@mountain-heritage.org).
