Needle Sports has supplied climbers and hill walkers from its shop on Keswick's Main Street since 1990, when it was founded by Stephen Reid and opened by the pre-war climbing pioneer Ivan Waller. Its name comes from Napes Needle, the famous rock pinnacle below Great Gable whose first ascent helped give birth to the sport of rock climbing. The shop stocks technical climbing hardware, mountaineering boots, clothing and guidebooks, and has built a reputation as one of the UK's leading specialist gear retailers, with a long-standing policy of employing only experienced climbers and walkers.

As a supporter of Mountain Heritage Trust, Needle Sports helps us preserve and share the stories of British climbing and mountaineering — the same heritage that runs through the crags and fells on its Lake District doorstep.
