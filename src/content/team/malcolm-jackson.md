Malcolm is a semi-retired scientist living in the Lakes. His main work has been in medical research, as a Professor and previously Associate Pro-Vice-Chancellor for Research and Impact at the University of Liverpool, with a substantial research interest in ageing and why we grow weaker as we get older. This has involved studies in cells, people and animals, including experiments examining the rapid loss of muscle force and strength that occurs in space on the International Space Station. His mountaineering interests developed as he grew up in the western Lakes, and have included rock climbing and hill walking across much of the UK as well as alpine ascents of many 4,000m peaks. He has a strong interest in mountaineering history and is delighted to be able to build on it to help the Trust in its mission to celebrate and share the heritage of British mountaineering and climbing.
